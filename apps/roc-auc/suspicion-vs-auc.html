8: Suspicion vs Model Quality

← Back to Visualizations

Higher clinical suspicion means moving along a single ROC curve by changing the probability threshold. Better prediction means a model with a higher AUC, which shifts the entire ROC curve upward and left.

Use the animation to see the difference between sliding the threshold on one curve and improving the model so that the whole curve moves closer to the ideal top-left corner.

What you are seeing

Red dot moving along orange curve: changing the threshold (higher or lower suspicion) on the same model.

Blue curve appearing above the orange curve: a better prediction model with higher AUC at every threshold.
